Phishing is a cyberattack where criminals send fake emails or messages to trick people into revealing passwords, bank details, or other personal information. A brute force attack happens when attackers use automated tools to guess many passwords until they find the correct one. A man-in-the-middle (MITM) attack occurs when a hacker secretly intercepts communication between two users, allowing them to view, steal, or modify data being exchanged. These attacks are common online threats that can lead to identity theft, financial loss, or unauthorized access to accounts.
